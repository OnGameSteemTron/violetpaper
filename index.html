Overview

It took more than 35 years for the global games business to grow to $35 billion in 2007, the year that the iPhone was introduced. Since then, the games market has added an extra $100 billion in revenues to arrive at this years total of $137.9 billion worldwide.

The uptake of smartphones has been a key contributor to the accelerated growth of the games market, in terms of both engagement and revenues, but is only one of the many factors that have brought us to where we are today.

To put current and future trends into perspective, we look back at the fundamental changes that have reshaped the industry in recent years. As we look toward the future, we foresee games playing an increasingly impactful role in disrupting and reshaping traditional industries, from implementing individual game mechanics to an array of mergers and acquisitions crossing the boundaries of traditional industries.
GAMING
AN ACCELERATED Growth for a decade
The way games are run has changed completely in only 10 years, from both an organizational and a business perspective, regardless of platform. Add to that the ongoing global alignment of distribution channels, franchises, and business models and it becomes clear that this is more than several individual trends happening simultaneously. Ultimately, the consumer has determined the pace of change. No other form of entertainment or media gives as much power to the consumer as games. Today, not only do games empower people to actively participate, but allow them to enjoy their passion for gaming in ways that suit any mood, interest, lifestyle, location, and budget. The viewing experience is part of games DNA. Almost any new game includes competitive modes that could lead to a professional esports scene, including live events, pro-gamer heroes, and teams with millions of fans. The global games industry has reinvented itself to maximize consumer engagement and revenue growth on a global scale. It is time to do away with old terminology and re-examine how we segment consumers based on the wide variety of game behavior and preferences.

PROBLEM
BEHIND THE gaming industry
In many ways, the industry is turning into a "recurring revenue model" and capitalizing on increased demand. Greenberg said: The initial purchase of a game is just the beginning of a game's monetization". While this is true and the gaming industry generating billions of revenue, including the Top 5 Most popular gaming websites which count a number of unique visitors of 70M users per month, many (pro) gamers and active actors of the gaming world still lack to get revenue from their work and their contents.

To be able to earn a salary from video gaming, the gamer can for the best, get into a team of pro-gamers and start a career in e-sports. The e-sports gamers are the new rock stars and they are able to make children dream, just how it is with traditional sports celebrities, yet the odd of becoming an e-sport athlete is very low and for most gamers it isnt possible to realize that dream. Thats why gamers and gaming content creators turn towards other alternatives to earn a salary from the gaming world but those alternatives involve lots of difficulties, time waste and most of the time discouragement.
Live Streams
Writing guides
Podcasts or videos
Competitive gaming
Games journalism
Games testing
All of these options are attractive to gamers but most of the time, even after choosing one path, the player wont get much. Even if the players contents are very good, nothing can assure a good revenue to live from the passion. Nothing can guarantee that the player will build a large and wide audience to get paid for everything he has done for the video game industry.

BLOCKCHAIN
A SOCIAL AND UTILITARIAN TECHNOLOGY FOR GAMING

OnGame taps into the power of two blockchains to incentivize engagement and offers an alternative, complementary way to monetize gaming content. Ongame uses the social protocol of STEEM as the core layer, combined with the power of TRON as the blockchain solution, to develop and implement different types of smart contracts.

The scalability of STEEM and TRON has been proven over the last few years. They are both adapted to OnGame development needs to make the platform accessible, secure and powerful, while allowing gamers to be rewarded with OnGame tokens (OG).

  • OnGame has no predefined algorithms.
  • A single account permit to communicate with both blockchains.
  • It is a free to use social gaming platform allowing creators of varying skills to have their own community.
